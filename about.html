Christopher D. Powers, Owner of Powers Photos

Chris Powers wears many hats: U.S. Navy veteran, volunteer firefighter, and heavy equipment operator. But the role he is most passionate about in life is photographer. Chris became interested in photography through an introductory course at community college. His first tools of the trade were a second-hand single reflex camera, some Kodak Tri-X film, and all the darkroom room time he could schedule. Since then, Chris has traded up to a Canon EOS 60D digital SLR and an iPhone 8.


During his 20 years on active duty with the Navy, Chris had the opportunity to not only travel the world several times over, but also capture images that represented the diversity of cultures across the globe. This was especially true when he spent time in the Pacific Rim while serving in locations such as Japan, Hong Kong, Thailand, and Australia. By portraying the people and places of the communities he visited, Chris developed his signature style that features an asymmetrical, yet dynamic composition, a pungent sense of color, and a dramatic contrast of dark and light. Chris is particularly well known for his images of natural landscapes, tourist destinations, and cityscapes at night.


The "postcard quality" of Chris' work has drawn admirers throughout his many years of travels. While most of his photography arises through a spontaneous process, Chris will also create images for clients by private commission.
